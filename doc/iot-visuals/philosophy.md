# Operational Circuit

Operational Circuit presents automation as a disciplined chain of permission, command, feedback, and accountability. The design language is intentionally aligned with the architecture visuals: measured grids, modular blocks, restrained color, and direct flow lines that make invisible service calls feel physically traceable. Every element should appear meticulously crafted, as if the diagram was refined through many passes by someone fluent in both system operations and visual hierarchy.

Space separates human action from scheduled automation. User-initiated control sits as a clear permission path, while background jobs and staff tools occupy their own operational layer. The composition uses cool blue for application interfaces, green for trusted data and access checks, violet for automation, and neutral grey for infrastructure or external messaging. The result must look like the product of deep expertise rather than a decorative sketch.

Text remains sparse and factual. API names, schedules, credentials, and outcomes are labels, not paragraphs. The final images should reward close reading without overwhelming the report page, using painstaking attention to alignment, consistent sizing, and balanced whitespace.

The central visual idea is that devices only respond after authority is established. Home Assistant, Sonoff, LINE messaging, and Django are not isolated objects; they are ordered segments of a controlled circuit. The final work should be clean enough for executive presentation and precise enough for technical review.
